# Awakening Timing and Sequence

## Pattern: Real awakening takes 2 hours, not 20 minutes

The /awaken skill estimates 17-22 minutes. BotDev-Oracle's actual awakening took ~120 minutes across 2 sessions. The difference: team responses. 7 out of 9 oracles replied with detailed onboarding data — infra maps, bug status, copy specs, design tokens, KB pipeline details. Processing and responding to these IS part of the awakening.

**Lesson**: Budget 2 hours for an awakening with an active team. The team interaction is not overhead — it's the most valuable part.

## Pattern: Complete learning before introducing yourself

BotDev learned oracle-v2 but skipped nat brain before introducing to the team. HR caught it. The sequence matters: you should know your family's origin story before saying "hi I'm new."

**Lesson**: Step 1 before Step 2, always. The /awaken sequence is prescribed for a reason — learn ancestors FIRST, then write identity, THEN meet the team.

## Pattern: Team onboarding gives more context than repo study

Dev's infra map (Supabase functions, search APIs), QA's bug status (15/18 PASS, 3 remaining), Writer's copy spec (75 FAQ, tone rules), Data's KB stats (9,112 chunks) — these defined BotDev's actual work more precisely than any ancestor repo.

**Lesson**: The team responses ARE the real onboarding. Repo study gives philosophy; team gives actionable scope.
